History

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) is a public research university in Little Rock, Arkansas. Established as Little Rock Junior College by the Little Rock School District in 1927, the institution became a private four-year university under the name Little Rock Univeristy in 1957. It returned to public status in 1969 when it merged with the university of Arkansas System under its present name. The former campus of Little Rock Junior College is now (2019) the campus of Philander Smith Univeristy.

At 250 acres (100ha), the UA Little Rock campus encompasses more than 56 buildings, including the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Science, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and Ottenheimer Library. Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities that include a learning resource center, art galleries, KUAR public radio station, University Television, and a campus-wide wireless network. It is classified among"R2: Doctoral Universities - High research activity".

Academics

The university features more than 100 undergraduate degress and 60 graduate degress, including graduate certificates, master's degrees, and doctorates, through both traditional and online courses. Students attend classes in one of teh university's three new colleges and a law school:

  • College of Business, Health, and Human Services
  • College of Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences, and Education
  • Donaghey College of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics
  • William H. Bowen School of Law

Student Life

Student Body Composition (as of May 2, 2022)

  • White: 48%
  • Black: 27%
  • Other: 15%
  • Hispanic: 4%
  • Foreign National: 3%
  • Asian: 2%
  • Native American: 1%

The student life at UA Little Rock is typical of public universities in the United States. it is characterized by student-run organizations and affiliation groups that support social, academic, athletic, and religious activities and interests. Services offered by the Office of Campus Life include intramural sports and fitness programs, diversity programs, leadership development, peer tutoring, student government, student support programs for non-traditional and first-generation students, a student-run newspaper, and fraternity and sorority life. The proximity of the campus to downtown Little Rock enables students to access recreational, entertainment, educational, internship, and employment opportunities unique to the area.

Campus Living

UA Little Rock provides a variety of on-campus living options for students, raning from traditional resident rooms to multiple-bedroom apartments. The university has four residence halls on the eastern side of the campus and the University Village Apartment Complex on the southern side. Six learning communities are available to students, focusing on:

  • Criminal Justice
  • Arts and Culture
  • Majors and Careers
  • Future Business Innovators
  • Nursing Careers
  • STEM

Athletics

UA Little Rock's 14 athletic teams are known as the Little Rocks Trojans, with almost all teams participating in the Sun Belt Conference. The university does not sponsor football; it last fielded a team in 1955 as Little Rock Junior College. Athletic offices are located in the Jack Stephens Center.

Sports Offered

  • Baseball
  • Men's and Women's Basketball
  • Men's and Women's Golf
  • Women's Volleyball
  • Women's Soccer
  • Women's Swimming and Diving
  • Men's and Women's Cross Country
  • Men's and Women's Track and Field (Indoor and Outdoor)
  • Men's Wrestling

Two teams compete outside the Sun Belt: Women's Swimming and Diving (Missouri Valley Conference) and Wrestling (Pac-12 Conference). Wrestling, the school's newest sport, began in 2019 and is the first Division I program in Arkansas.

Little Rock will move to the Ohio Valley Conference for the 2022-23 season.

Collections and Archives

On July 1, 2014, the UA Little Rock Collections and Archives division was created. The division encompasses:

  • Ottenheimer Library
  • Center for Arkansas History and Culture
  • Sequouah National Research Center
